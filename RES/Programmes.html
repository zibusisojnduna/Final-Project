Our Curriculum

Our curriculum is split into two parts. From Grade 1 to Grade 3 our students work with the D-CAPS curriculum. From Grade 4 to Grade 5 our students focus mor on developing thier skills and learning new ones.

What is D-CAPS?

D-CAPS is an abbreviation for Differentiated Curriculum And Assessment Policy Statement. At its core it is the CAPS system used across South Africa but in this case it is slightly adjusted for our students with disabilies. This programme is structured in such a way that it makes provisions for a large scale of learners with moderate to severe intellectual disabilty and learning difficulties across Grade 1 to 3. The other goal is to help develop their perosnality, talents and creativity, as well as ther mental and physical abilities.

Skills Development

From Grade 4 to Grade 5 our students mostly focus on improving skills and developing new ones.These include the likes of leatherwork, woodwork and printing. The students also take part in the agricultrural side of skills including gardening, sewing and garmet making. This is done in the hopes that they can be prepared for life outside if the classroom.

Woodwork

Woodworking is a rewarding and fulfilling hobby that can produce beautiful and useful items for your home or as gifts for friends and family. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced woodworker, there's always something new to learn and skills to improve.A woodworking project makes an excellent sense for any student with an appreciation for nature. Building these projects can increase the confidence of school students and encourage them to do some technical work.In addition, they are a great way to use small wood scrap and save money for the students.

Printing

Printing enables students to annotate and take notes directly on physical copies, facilitating personalised learning experiences. The ability to write, highlight, and underline on printed materials aids in the organisation of information and enhances critical thinking skills.

Gardenning

In a school garden, school children learn how to cultivate flowers and vegetable gardens. The school garden is an extension of regular school work. It is an effort to get children outdoors and away from books. It is a healthy, practical learning activity which adds interest and exercise.Benefits are: It teaches students about science, nutrition, and sustainability. It encourages experiential learning and inquiry-based learning. It increases students' openness to, preference for, and intake of fruits and vegetables. It improves students' academic performance and attitudes towards learning. It connects students with nature and improves their behavior at home and school.
